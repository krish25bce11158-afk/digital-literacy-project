# Cybercrime Case Study – Phishing Attack

Phishing is one of the most common types of cybercrime where attackers try to trick people into sharing personal or financial information. In this type of fraud, the attacker usually sends fake emails, messages, or links that appear to come from trusted organizations such as banks, online shopping websites, or government services.

For example, a student may receive an email claiming that their bank account needs verification. The email may contain a link that looks genuine but actually leads to a fake website. When the student enters their login details, the attacker collects the information and gains access to the account. This can lead to financial loss, identity theft, or misuse of personal data.

Students are often targeted because they frequently use online payment apps, social media, and emails. Many users are not aware of how to identify fake messages or suspicious links. The consequences of phishing attacks can include loss of money, privacy issues, and misuse of personal identity.

Understanding phishing helps students stay alert and avoid sharing confidential information on untrusted websites.
